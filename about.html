Our Vision

Currently, South Africa has no formal, nationally recognised minimum standards or guidelines governing practice in companion animal shelters. This absence has left the sector fragmented, under-supported and vulnerable, despite the extraordinary dedication of those working within it. This year in 2026 animal shelters are establishing minimum standards. These new guidelines, while providing even better welfare for the animals, will require great funding to achieve widespread. At this very moment, animal welfare is topical and on the brink of change which requires funding.

Paw Prints serves to assist South Africa’s social issue regarding animal welfare for domesticated species. Rather than proposing yet another animal shelter or pop-up veterinary clinic, the business serves to attract and channel funding to approved, established shelters to assist in the re-establishment and upkeep of the new standard of animal shelter in discussion above.
